Great Managers Aren't Born. They're Built One Conversation at a Time.

70%
Workplace Leadership Data

of the variance in team engagement traces back to the manager.

When managers grow, teams change. When they don't, neither does the work.

Source · Gallup · State of the American Manager

This month, SPI introduces a Leadership Development catalog, practical courses for people leaders (and those that follow them) on the conversations that shape how the work gets done.

The hardest parts of leadership rarely show up on a Gantt chart. They appear in the feedback that never gets shared, the conflict that goes unaddressed, or the team member who's quietly disengaging while everyone assumes they're fine.

Leadership Development in SPI is built for those moments—the conversations between the milestones that often determine whether a team grows, adapts, and succeeds.

These short, scenario-based courses focus on real leadership challenges and practical communication skills. Because in leadership, what you say—and how you say it—can shape trust, performance, engagement, and outcomes long after the conversation ends.

Action Required · Policy Update

Required 1:1s Shift to Bi-Weekly, Effective June 8.

To better balance manager capacity while maintaining consistency and accountability, required 1:1 meetings transition from a weekly to a bi-weekly cadence on June 8, 2026. Weekly cadence remains an option, and stays required for new hires and performance-managed employees through their first six months.

Standard Cadence
All Managers & Direct Reports
Bi-Weekly
Effective June 8, 2026
Weekly Cadence
New Hires & Performance Management
Required
Through first 6 months

Manager Responsibilities

Schedule and maintain regular 1:1s. Provide timely coaching, feedback, and follow-up. Discuss priorities, workload, performance, and development. Document key topics and next steps.

Direct Report Responsibilities

Come prepared with updates, questions, and topics. Surface concerns, challenges, and roadblocks proactively. Discuss career development goals. Follow through on agreed-upon action items.

How to Access Log into Rippling, then Talent, then 1:1s. Set the SN 1:1 Template as your default via the three-dot menu, top-right of the 1:1 page.
